This web page computes institutional publication metrics for computer science configured by users. These metrics are principally focused on institutions, as opposed to faculty or authors, and for use in assessing both the past and predicting future research impact based on publications. The data and scripts are available here. We welcome corrections and feedback. See more on motivation and methodology.

Introduction to the data and tool

We cleaned publication, venue, and institution data from DBLP and Microsoft Academic for 2007 to 2016 appearing in 200-FIXME venues from 400-FIXME institutions. For each paper in this corpus we divide credit equally among all authors and their institutions at time of publication. A publication never changes institutions and all authors (graduate students, post docs, faculty, undergraduates, etc.) accrue credit to their institution.

Users combine a retrospective and a prospective metric --- measured (retrospective) and prediction (prospective) --- to compute an institutional measure. The measured metric uses citation counts and includes all citations at any time to papers published in the specified year range. The prediction metric counts papers in an independently specified year range and weighs them by venue. We suggest a disjoint range for the two metrics and that prediction only be used on recent publications that have not had sufficient time to accrue citations (e.g., 2014-2016).

Users select all topics or individual topics to determine venues and may exclude/include individual venues to create custom venue lists. Users select year ranges for each metric and a venue weight. A venue weight of one values all venues equally. The geometric mean venue weight assigns each venue the geometric mean of citations to papers appearing in the venue from 2007â€”2016, thus more highly cited venues weigh more. (Venue weight is displayed next to selected venues.) Since the metrics are not comparable, the combined metric uses geometric mean, where the user assigns a relative weight (alpha) to the measured and predictive metrics.
